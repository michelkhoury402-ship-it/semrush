04 — The Five Gaps No One Is Talking About

Where product marketing has the highest-impact opportunity

The Evidence

Price is the #1 complaint across Trustpilot, G2, and Capterra. Reviewers consistently say "great tool, too expensive." Yet the $50K+ segment (those who grasp the full value) is growing 74% YoY.

Why This Matters

Every prospect who churns after a trial because they think "$140/month is a lot for keyword research" is a product marketing failure. They never understood they were buying a visibility operating system.

The Evidence

89% of Semrush's 28M+ users are on the free plan and never convert. Multiple reviewers describe it as "overwhelming" and "not beginner-friendly." The cancellation process requires 5+ screens, generating disproportionate negative sentiment.

Why This Matters

Most users need 3 to 5 tools out of 50+. The job is to guide them there fast, prove value in the first session, and make the rest a growth path, not a source of confusion.

The Evidence

The toolkit doesn't yet track Claude (Anthropic), full Perplexity coverage, or Meta AI. Multiple 2026 reviews call these out specifically. Missing major LLMs is an objection in every enterprise conversation.

Why This Matters

In category creation, perceived comprehensiveness matters as much as actual capability. Frame coverage as "the widest and fastest-expanding" rather than claiming completeness.

The Evidence

Semrush One vs. SEO Classic vs. standalone toolkits vs. App Center vs. Enterprise. Seats cost $45 to $100 plus $99/user for AI Visibility. One analysis noted adding a team member can "easily double the cost."

Why This Matters

Confusion at the point of purchase kills conversion. Product marketing should own the packaging narrative: simplify communication, make the recommended path unmissable, address seat pricing transparently.

The Evidence

Support is the #1 operational weakness across all review platforms. The AI chatbot fails on basic queries. Human support reportedly only available during US hours. Refund policy described as "extremely rigid."

Why This Matters

Product marketing can reduce dependency through self-service enablement, better docs, community forums, and proactive education. Shift perception from "they don't help" to "they make it easy."

05 — My Product Marketing Framework

The Visibility Positioning Engine

Every initiative should serve at least two of these four lenses simultaneously. This is a system for prioritization, not a checklist.

◇

Narrative Architecture

What story does the market tell about us vs. the one we want?

Move from "all-in-one SEO tool" to "the platform that makes brands discoverable everywhere." The Adobe acquisition makes this reframing both urgent and possible.

△

Buyer Enablement

How easy is it to understand, believe, and buy with confidence?

ROI calculator, persona-based buying guides, simplified pricing, transparent competitive positioning, frictionless trial-to-paid. Every dollar making it easier to buy returns more than a dollar generating traffic.

○

Customer Success Marketing

How do we turn paying customers into expanding advocates?

With 104% NRR, there's decent retention but limited expansion. Drive cross-toolkit adoption, celebrate wins publicly, build a certification ecosystem that makes Semrush expertise a career asset.

□

Competitive Moat Storytelling

How do we make structural advantages feel inevitable?

Stop defending individual features. Tell a systems story: "Only Semrush connects keyword intelligence, competitive analysis, content optimization, AI visibility, and campaign execution. That connection is where insight lives."
